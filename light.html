Light

The electromagnetic radiation within the portion of the electromagnetic spectrum that is perceived by the human eye.

Core Concepts

The fundamental principles of optics and the nature of light.

Properties of Light

Light travels in straight lines, can be reflected and refracted, and exhibits wave-particle duality.

Color

The perception of different wavelengths of light. White light is a mixture of all colors, as seen in a rainbow.

Reflection and Refraction

Reflection is the bouncing of light off a surface. Refraction is the bending of light as it passes through different media.

Light Waves

Light behaves as a transverse electromagnetic wave, characterized by its wavelength, frequency, and speed.

Light Emission

Light is produced by atoms when electrons transition between energy levels, releasing energy as photons.

Light Quanta (Photons)

Light also behaves as a particle, a discrete packet of energy called a photon, which is the basis of quantum optics.

The Illuminated Universe

Light is central to our experience of the world, from the sunlight that powers life on Earth to the starlight that reveals the vastness of the cosmos. As a form of electromagnetic radiation, it is a fascinating subject that bridges classical optics and modern quantum physics. Its study has led to profound discoveries about the nature of reality itself.

The dual nature of light—behaving as both a continuous wave and a discrete particle (photon)—was a key puzzle that led to the development of quantum mechanics. Understanding light is fundamental to nearly every field of science and technology, from creating lenses and lasers to building fiber-optic networks and exploring the universe with powerful telescopes.
