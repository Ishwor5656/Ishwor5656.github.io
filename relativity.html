Theory of Relativity

Einstein's revolutionary theories that reshaped our understanding of space, time, gravity, and the universe.

Core Concepts

The foundational ideas of Special and General Relativity.

Special Theory of Relativity

Describes the relationship between space and time for objects moving at constant speeds, in the absence of gravity.

General Theory of Relativity

A theory of gravitation where gravity is a geometric property of spacetime, which is curved by mass and energy.

Reshaping the Cosmos

Albert Einstein's theory of relativity, presented in two parts, fundamentally altered our perception of the universe. Special relativity (1905) unified space and time into a single entity, spacetime, and showed that measurements of time and distance are not absolute but depend on the observer's motion. It also introduced the most famous equation in physics, E=mcÂ², revealing the deep connection between mass and energy.

General relativity (1915) went further, describing gravity not as a force, but as the curvature of spacetime caused by the mass and energy within it. This elegant theory explains the orbits of planets, the bending of light from distant stars, and the existence of cosmic phenomena like black holes and gravitational waves. Together, these theories form one of the most significant intellectual achievements in human history.
