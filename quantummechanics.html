Quantum Mechanics

The branch of physics that describes the behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic levels.

Core Concepts

The mind-bending principles that govern the subatomic world.

Wave-Particle Duality

The fundamental property of quantum entities to exhibit both particle and wave characteristics.

Quantum States and Wave Function

Describes the state of a quantum system; the wave function, Ψ, contains all measurable information.

Schrödinger Equation

The fundamental equation that describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time.

Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

A core principle stating the limit to precision with which pairs of properties can be simultaneously known.

Quantum Superposition

A system can exist in a combination of multiple states at once until it is measured.

Quantum Entanglement

Linked particles affect each other's state instantly, regardless of the distance separating them.

Operators and Observables

Physical quantities (observables) are represented by mathematical operators that act on the wave function.

Quantum Tunneling

A particle can pass through a potential barrier that it classically shouldn't have enough energy to overcome.

Quantization of Energy Levels

Physical properties like energy and charge exist in discrete, indivisible units called "quanta."

Spin and Angular Momentum

An intrinsic form of angular momentum carried by elementary particles, with no classical analogue.

Quantum Statistics

Describes the statistical behavior of systems of particles (Bose–Einstein or Fermi–Dirac statistics).

Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

A model system in quantum mechanics, foundational for understanding vibrations, fields, and more.

Hydrogen Atom and Atomic Models

The quantum mechanical model of the hydrogen atom was a key success of the theory.

Perturbation Theory

A method for finding approximate solutions to a problem by starting from an exact solution of a related one.

Pauli Exclusion Principle

No two identical fermions can occupy the same quantum state simultaneously within a quantum system.

Dirac Equation

A relativistic quantum mechanical equation that describes the behavior of electrons and predicted antimatter.

Quantum Field Theory (QFT)

The theoretical framework that combines classical field theory, special relativity, and quantum mechanics.

Applications of Quantum Mechanics

Powers modern technology, including lasers, semiconductors, medical imaging (MRI), and quantum computing.

The Realm of Probability

Where classical mechanics offers certainty, quantum mechanics presents a world built on probability and uncertainty. It is the physics of the very small: atoms, electrons, and photons. The rules that govern this realm are counter-intuitive and defy our everyday experience, yet they are essential for modern technology, powering everything from lasers and computers to medical imaging.

Unlike the deterministic clockwork of the classical universe, the quantum world operates on probabilities. We can't know with certainty where a particle is, only where it is likely to be. This probabilistic nature, encapsulated by the wave function, is not a limitation of our tools but a fundamental aspect of reality itself, challenging our deepest intuitions about the universe.
